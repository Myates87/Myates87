How to make Beaded dangle earrings

This project is a great place to start learning the art of jewlery making.
It isn't time consuming and it's simple so let's get right to it!

Jewelry and Tools you'll need.

  • Round-nose pliers
  • Wire cutters
  • Head pins or 20 gauge wire
  • A set of beads
  • Ear wire

If you want, you can make your own ear wire

STEP 1:
Choose your beads!

Be creative, pick any style of beads that catches your eye. Be cautious when it comes to the weight of the bead.
Wearing to heavy of earring is not fun. It can damage and stretch your piercing.

STEP 2:

Pick out your pair of ear wires. There are many styles of ear wire to choose from, or you could even make your own ear wire.

STEP 3:

To make headpin, cut two 3” pieces of 20- gauge wire. If you bought pre-made headpins skip this prep and lay out two headpins.

STEP 4:

With your round-nose plyers, create a loop at one end if each piece of 20-gauge wire. If you have pre-made headpins skip this part.

STEP 5:

Take your wire-cutters and trim the excess wire from loop. Grab your pliers and squeeze the trimmed loop.

STEP 6:

Slide your bead onto your headpin, and create the same loop as we did on step 4.

STEP 7:

Now that you have formed the final loop, grab the end of the excess wire with the pliers. I like to use the round nose pliers to hold the loop still.

Wrap the wire three times around the stem of the loop (between the loop and the top of the bead).

STEP 8:

Take your wire-cutters and trim the excess wire from single wrapped loop.

STEP 9:

Use your chain-nose plyers to attach the earring to an ear-wire.

Repeat this process and we’re done!  You can use this technique to make many different styles of jewelry.
